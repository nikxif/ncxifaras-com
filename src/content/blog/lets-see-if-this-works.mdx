This is my first time trying out my new posting system!

In making this, I had one major rule: no overengineering. Well, I might have *under*engineered a bit because this is like typing in the terminal. I feel like my audience is my own kernel.<Sidenote>If you feel the need to tell me the terminal doesn't talk to the kernel, please rethink your outlook on life.</Sidenote>

Still, it is better than having different apps for every little job that needs doing, isn't it? That brings semi-nostalgic images of kitchens in the 00s, full of one-use kitchen gadgets (often telemarketing products that found their forever home in my grandma's cupboard) that are only good at __one__ specific task, and, more often than not, not even _that_ good at it.

Mindful as I am of my digital ecosystem becoming an across-time-and-space counterpart of those dark cupboards where those lumps of plastic go to die, and in fear that a pile of barely-used unitasker apps are going to fall on my head like egg-slicers and herb scissors balancing on top of tupperware containers<Sidenote>I'm convinced these same containers have been beamed in into everyone's kitchen, _a la_ Terminator.</Sidenote>, I took the step. No more GUI. Just words. Letters and symbols, and nothing more.

Well, I'm on paragraph four, and what can I say? I like it.

Writing in a simple, distraction-free, monospace, white-on-black (for me) environment is gracefully luxurious in a way. It reminds me of having only a pen and a piece of paper, and my mind bursting with ideas and concepts I wanted to make real by jotting them down. God knows I eventually wrote so few of them.

Anyways, this is getting a bit long for a test post. Let's leave it here. Ta da.
